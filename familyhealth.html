Goals, Objectives, Vision, Mission, Intended Target, and Audience

Goal

To provide universal access to FP information and services whenever and wherever these are needed.

Objectives

The FP program addresses the need to help couples and individuals achieve their desired family size within the context of responsible parenthood and improve their reproductive health.

Vision

Empowered men and women living healthy, productive, and fulfilling lives and exercising the right to regulate their fertility through legally and acceptable family planning services.

Mission

The DOH in partnership with LGUs, NGOs, private sectors, and communities ensures the availability of FP information and services to men and women who need them.

Intended/Target Audience

Men and women of reproductive age (15-49 years old) including adolescents.

Benefits of Family Planning



Promotes Women's Health:


  • Reduces maternal mortality and morbidity by spacing pregnancies and preventing unintended pregnancies.
  • Enables women to plan and prepare for pregnancies, reducing health risks associated with pregnancies that are too closely spaced or occur at an older age.


Improves Child Health:


  • Helps in spacing births, allowing mothers to recover and ensuring healthier outcomes for infants.
  • Reduces infant mortality and improves child development by ensuring adequate maternal and child healthcare.


Empowers Individuals and Couples:


  • Provides individuals with the autonomy to make informed decisions about their reproductive health and future.
  • Supports gender equality by empowering women to pursue education, careers, and other life goals.


Reduces Poverty:


  • Enables families to invest more resources in each child's education, health, and well-being.
  • Helps break the cycle of poverty by allowing families to plan for economic stability and reduce financial strain.


Three Important Elements of Family Planning



Family Spacing


  • Ensuring adequate time between pregnancies allows mothers to recover physically and emotionally, reducing the risk of maternal complications and improving the chances of healthier birth outcomes. Additionally, spacing pregnancies can enable families to better provide for the needs of each child and invest in their education and well-being.
  • Teenage mothers have much shorter birth intervals (19 months) than mothers age 20-29 (25 months) or mothers age 30-39 (34 months).
  • 3 years gap is recommended for the health of both mother and infant


Proper Timing


  • Timing is essential in family planning decisions, where factors such as financial stability, education, and career aspirations play significant roles. Many Filipino couples strive to achieve a balance between having children and pursuing personal and professional goals. Moreover, considering the economic conditions and job opportunities available can influence decisions about when to start a family and how many children to have.
  • The recommended interval before attempting the next pregnancy is at least 24 months in order to reduce risks to the mother and infant.


Number of Pregnancies


  • socio-economic factors such as poverty, access to education, and healthcare services can influence decisions about family size. While the Catholic Church's teachings on contraception can impact contraceptive use, the government has been promoting access to family planning services to empower couples to make informed choices about their reproductive health.


Types of Family Planning



Natural:


Natural family planning methods rely on understanding a woman's menstrual cycle to determine fertile and non-fertile days. These methods do not involve the use of any medications or devices. The effectiveness of natural family planning methods can vary based on the consistency and accuracy of tracking menstrual cycles.


  • Calendar/Rhythm Method
  • Basal Body Temperature (BBT) Method
  • Cervical Mucus Method




Barrier:


Barrier methods of family planning involve using physical barriers to prevent sperm from reaching the egg. These methods are reversible and offer protection against sexually transmitted infections (STIs).


  • Condoms (male and female)
  • Diaphragm
  • Cervical Cap


Surgical:


Surgical family planning methods are intended to be permanent and irreversible forms of contraception. These procedures involve surgical interventions to block or cut the fallopian tubes (for women) or vas deferens (for men) to prevent the union of sperm and egg.


  • Tubal Ligation (for women)
  • Vasectomy (for men)


Pharmaceutical:


Hormonal Methods:


  • Birth Control Pills: Oral contraceptives containing hormones (estrogen and/or progestin) to prevent ovulation.
  • Contraceptive Patch: A patch worn on the skin that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.
  • Birth Control Injection: Injectable contraceptives (e.g., Depo-Provera) administered every few months to prevent pregnancy by suppressing ovulation.


Intrauterine Device (IUD):


  • Hormonal IUD: A small, T-shaped device inserted into the uterus that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.
  • Copper IUD: A non-hormonal IUD that contains copper, which creates an inflammatory reaction in the uterus, preventing pregnancy.


Implantable Devices:


  • Birth Control Implant: A small rod implanted under the skin of the upper arm that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy for several years.
  • Copper Intrauterine Device (IUD): Can be used as emergency contraception if inserted within a few days after unprotected intercourse.


Behavioral Methods:


  • Withdrawal (Pull-Out) Method: Involves the man withdrawing his penis from the vagina before ejaculation.
  • Abstinence: Choosing not to engage in sexual intercourse.



Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Magastos ba ang mga family planning methods?

Ang karamihan ng mga family planning methods ay mabibili sa mga botika sa abot kayang halaga. Sa ating mga rural health unit naman, ito ay libreng serbisyo. may mga kompanya naman na nagbibigay ng mga FP methods na hiyang sa kanilang mga empleyado.

Nakakasama ba sa kalusugan ang family planning methods?

Hindi. Lahat ng family planning methods ay ligtas at mabisa Kung Tama ang paggamit. Ang mag-asawa ay maaring pumili ng paraan na hiyang sa kanilang katawan, at angkop sa kanilang pangangailangan at kalusugan.

Ang family planning ba ay isang uri ng aborsyon?

Hindi. Ang aborsyon ay ang paglaglag ng ipinagbubuntis. Ang family planning naman ay ang pag-iwas sa pagbubuntis. Sa family planning, maiiwasan ang aborsyon dahil natutulungan ang mga asawa na maiwasan ang wala sa planong pagbubuntis.

Pwede rin ba sa lalaki ang mga family planning sevices?

Oo. Ang mga lalaki ay may mga pangangailangan din sa pagpaplano ng pamilya na hindi lamang nakakaapekto sa kanilang kalusugan sa sekswal at reproductive, ngunit maaari ding magkaroon ng epekto sa kanilang pangkalahatang kalusugan.

Will my family planning services be kept confidential?

Birth control and other reproductive health services at Family Planning clinics are confidential, which means that clinic staff cannot share your information with anyone unless you give the clinic staff permission

Do clients need to use another contraceptive method after a vasectomy?

Yes. For the first three (3) months after the procedure, there are still sperms in the semen after ejaculation. In that case, clients need to use another FP method until after three months when semen analysis results demonstrate absence of sperm. Not using FP method for the first 3 months has been the main cause of pregnancies among couples relying on vasectomy. Thereafter, vasectomy is highly effective

Will BTL make a woman lose her sexual desire?

No. After BTL a woman will look and feel the same as before. She may find sex more enjoyable because the fear of getting pregnant is no longer there.

Can implants affect mood and libido?

Mood and libido can be affected by changes in the relationship and not necessarily by the subdermal contraceptive implants.

Does the injectable cause miscarriage?

No. Research on combined contraceptives finds that they do not disrupt an existing pregnancy. They should not be used to try to cause an abortion. They will not do so. Should injectables fail to prevent pregnancy, there will be no adverse effects on the pregnancy.

How long does it take to become pregnant after stopping the pill?

Woman’s fertility return as soon as she stops taking oral contraceptives. She can become pregnant as quickly. The menstrual pattern she had prior to using the pill generally returns after she stops taking them. Some women may have to wait a few months before their usual menstrual pattern returns
