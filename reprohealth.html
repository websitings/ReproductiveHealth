Definition

Reproductive Health (RH)

  • Reproductive health is the crucial part of the overall health and is central to human development.
  • It is an important component of general health and is prerequisite for social, economic and human development.

Reproductive Health and Development

  • UNFPA works to ensure sexual and reproductive health and rights remain at the very center of development. The International Conference on Population and Development draws a clear connection between reproductive health, human rights and sustainable development.
  • When sexual and reproductive health needs are not met, individuals are deprived of the right to make crucial choices about their own bodies and futures, with a cascading impact on their families’ welfare and future generations.
  • Because women bear children, and also often bear the responsibility for nurturing them, sexual and reproductive health and rights issues cannot be separated from gender equality. Cumulatively, the denial of these rights exacerbates poverty and gender inequality.
  • Reproductive health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity, in all matters relating to the reproductive system and to its functions and processes.

Essential Components of Reproductive Health

  1. FAMILY PLANNING: counseling, information, education, communication and service (emphasizing prevention of unwanted pregnancies)
  2. SAFE MOTHERHOOD: education and services for healthy pregnancy, safe delivery, and postnatal care including breast feeding
  3. CARE OF THE NEWBORN
  4. PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT OF COMPLICATIONS OF ABORTION
  5. PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT OF RTI, STDs, HIV/AIDS and other reproductive health conditions.
  6. INFORMATION, EDUCATION AND COUNSELING as appropriate on human sexuality, reproductive health and responsible parenthood for individuals, couples and adolescents.
  7. PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT OF SUBFERTILITY
  8. LIFE CYCLE ISSUES INCLUDING BREAST CANCER, CANCER OF THE REPRODUCTIVE SYSTEM AND CARE OF THE ELDERLY.

Benefits of Family Planning, Sexual Health, and Maternal Health

Family Planning

People who are in good reproductive health can schedule and space out their pregnancies, which benefits both the mother and the kid. It gives them the information they need to decide how many children and when to have them.

Sexual Health

This includes a wide range of aspects, such as access to contraception, safe and consensual sex practices, and the prevention and treatment of STIs. This lowers the chance of infection and encourages healthy sexual relationships.

Maternal Health

Decreased mother Mortality, the risk of mother death is considerably decreased by receiving adequate prenatal, skilled delivery attendance, and postpartum care. Obstetric problems can be avoided and lives can be saved with prompt interventions and the availability of emergency services.

Laws and Legal Bases

\

The legal and policy framework for reproductive health, maternal health, and family planning in the Philippines is anchored on the Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Act of 2012, which aims to provide comprehensive reproductive health services while addressing societal challenges and controversies. Ongoing efforts are focused on overcoming barriers to implementation and ensuring universal access to reproductive health services for all Filipinos.


The key aspects of the legal and policy landscape concerning reproductive health, maternal health, and family planning in the Philippines includes:

The Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Act of 2012 (RA 10354)

This law, commonly known as the Reproductive Health (RH) Law, was enacted to promote universal access to reproductive health services, including family planning information and services, particularly for poor and marginalized sectors. It upholds the right to make free and informed decisions, respects the dignity of women, and ensures the health of mothers and children. The RH Law also mandates the integration of age-appropriate reproductive health education in schools.

Maternal Health Care Policies

The Philippines has policies specifically targeting maternal health, aimed at reducing maternal mortality and improving access to maternal care services. The Department of Health (DOH) implements programs that provide prenatal care, skilled attendance during childbirth, and postnatal care to pregnant women.

Family Planning Programs

Family planning services in the Philippines are guided by the RH Law and are integrated into the broader public health system. These programs offer a range of contraceptive methods and promote responsible parenthood.

Executive Order No. 12 (2017)

This executive order reinforces the implementation of the RH Law by mandating government agencies to intensify and accelerate the implementation of critical actions to address maternal mortality and morbidity, and to ensure universal access to quality family planning information and services.

International Commitments

The Philippines is a signatory to international agreements that promote reproductive health and maternal health rights, such as the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW) and the International Conference on Population and Development (ICPD) Programme of Action.

Challenges and Controversies

Despite legal provisions and policies, there are challenges in implementing reproductive health and family planning programs in the Philippines. Some sectors, including certain religious groups, oppose certain aspects of the RH Law, particularly those related to contraception and sex education in schools. This opposition has led to delays and limitations in fully implementing the law.

Role of NGOs and Civil Society

Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and civil society groups play a vital role in advocating for reproductive health and family planning rights in the Philippines. They provide services and support to marginalized communities and advocate for policy reforms to improve access to reproductive health services.

Important Human Rights Related to Reproductive Health


  • People are able to have a satisfying and safe sex life
  • They have the capability to reproduce and
  • Freedom to decide if, when and how often to do so.
  • Implicit above last condition are the rights of men and women to be informed and to have access to safe, effective, affordable and acceptable methods of family planning of their choice for regulation of fertility which are not against law
  • Right of access to appropriate health care services that will enable women to go safely through pregnancy and childbirth
  • Provide couples with the best chance of having a healthy infant
  • Also includes gender equality and equity, empowerment of women and the provision of universal access to appropriate health services over the life cycle.

Life Cycle Approach to Reproductive Health


  • RH not merely confined to the health of the individual at the age he/she is capable for reproduction
  • Life cycle approach recognized that each stage of life, from infancy to old age has reproductive needs
  • Health of an infant is largely dependent on the mother’s health status and access to health care during pregnancy, childbirth and immediate postpartum period.
